	\mathbf{x}[k+1] =& \overbrace{\left[
	\begin{array}{ccc}

	\begin{array}{ccc}

	\end{array}
	\right]}^\mathbf{C} \mathbf{x}[k] + v[k]

with the following disturbance and noise properties:
\begin{align*}
	\mathbf{E}[\mathbf{w}[k]] =& 0, \quad \mathbf{E}[\mathbf{w}[k]\mathbf{w}[l]] = \delta[k,l]\mathbf{Q} \\
	\mathbf{E}[v[k]] =& 0, \quad \mathbf{E}[v[k]v[l]] = \delta[k,l]\sigma^2

You are using a discrete time Kalman filter to estimate the states of the system. If the apriori covariance matrix at time $k$ is $P^-[k] = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{array} \right]$, what is the Kalman gain at time $k$?

            \answer  $\mathbf{L}[k] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1/(2 + \sigma^2) \\ 2/(2 + \sigma^2) \end{array} \right] $
            \correctanswer  $\mathbf{L}[k] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 2/(2 + \sigma^2) \\ 1/(2 + \sigma^2) \end{array} \right] $
